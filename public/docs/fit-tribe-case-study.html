The Brand
A high-volume activewear brand

Fit Tribe is an Egyptian activewear brand that built real traffic momentum on TikTok. By Q4 2025, they had 352K sessions and $170K in revenue — but TikTok traffic, which made up 40% of sessions, was converting at 0.1%. Their site wasn't built to absorb cold social traffic at scale.

Navigation was undifferentiated. Search was buried. Product pages didn't do the persuasion work needed for visitors who had zero prior brand familiarity. The cart lost people on mobile — which is where the majority of TikTok traffic lands.

Activewear Egyptian market 8-figure brand
The Challenge
TikTok drove 40% of traffic — and converted at 0.1%.

TikTok visitors arrived with zero purchase intent. As Fit Tribe scaled TikTok spend, sessions climbed but CVR dropped. TikTok had previously converted around 2% — by the time of the engagement it had fallen to 0.1%. TikTok made up 40% of total traffic, which dragged blended CVR to 1.16%.

The site had no mechanism to recover those visitors. Navigation was undifferentiated. Search was buried. Product pages didn't do persuasion work. The cart lost people on mobile.

"TikTok accounted for 40% of traffic and had a 0.1% conversion rate. That was the drag. Fix the site for cold traffic, and blended CVR goes up even as TikTok scales."

The Approach
We optimized product discovery and navigation.

30-day sprint. We audited the full funnel, found the highest-leverage gaps, and shipped the fixes. The focus: get TikTok visitors to the right product faster, and make the purchase path work for cold traffic on mobile.

Search visibility optimization
Search usage started at 0.08%. It wasn't discoverable enough for TikTok visitors who arrived looking for something specific.
0.08% search usage → significant adoption Higher CVR for search users
Color swatches to real image thumbnails
Replaced abstract color swatches on the collections page with real product images per variant.
Faster product decision-making Lower bounce on collection pages
Cart cross-sell optimization
Rebuilt the slide cart with upsells that don't disrupt checkout. Mobile-optimized to keep the path to purchase clean.
+2.85% revenue uplift +€400K per year
Category-first navigation
Rebuilt nav around product categories shoppers actually use — leggings, flared pants, tops, outerwear — instead of season or campaign tabs.
Reduced time to right product Lower exit rate from nav
PDP built for cold traffic
Rewrote the PDP persuasion layer: size guidance, social proof, UGC, and trust signals for visitors with no prior brand familiarity.
Higher add-to-cart rate Lower sizing-related drop-off
Filterable, scannable collections
Added size, color, and style filters. Restructured the grid layout. TikTok visitors landing on a category page now reach the right product in fewer taps.
Faster path to product Lower collection-page exit

The Results
Traffic Up 21%. Revenue Up 16%. CVR Held.

The comparison is Q4 2025 vs. Q1 2026. Sessions grew 21% — driven by more TikTok scaling. Blended CVR rose from 1.16% to 1.24% despite TikTok accounting for 40% of traffic and converting at 0.1%. TikTok was dragging the number down. Product discovery and navigation improvements pulled it back up.

After TikTok was removed from the channel mix, CVR rose to 1.86%. The optimization had compounded — the site was converting better on its own traffic, not just compensating for TikTok's drag.
